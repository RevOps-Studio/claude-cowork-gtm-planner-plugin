## Executive summary
Fluxen competes in the energy-management-system (EMS) space, where the
narrative is owned by industrial giants (Schneider EcoStruxure, Siemens
Desigo/SIMATIC Energy) selling suite-scale platforms to large enterprises,
and contested at the bottom by electrical integrators bundling generic
dashboards with installation work. Between both sits the real rival:
**doing nothing** — spreadsheets fed from utility bills, plus panic before
ISO audits. For a mid-size plant without an energy manager, the suites are
oversized and the integrator dashboards die after handover. That middle is
thinly served: a defensible space for a fast-deploy, audit-ready EMS —
if Fluxen can prove credibility without enterprise logos.

## Three-layer map
**Layer 1 — direct:** Schneider EcoStruxure Resource Advisor, Siemens
energy suites, Spacewell Energy (Dexma), Wattabit, local SaaS entrants.
**Layer 2 — category (same job, different offer):** electrical integrators
with white-label dashboards; energy consultancies doing periodic audits;
utilities' "free" monitoring portals.
**Layer 3 — non-competitive alternatives:** spreadsheets + utility bills
(the norm); hiring an energy manager (rare below 500 employees); doing
nothing until the audit. *Most lost deals in this segment go to layer 3.*

## Priority competitors (4)
1. **Schneider EcoStruxure** — category: enterprise energy & sustainability
   platform. Promise: end-to-end visibility at corporate scale. Apparent
   ICP: multinationals, multi-site portfolios. Strength: brand authority —
   the "safe choice" (source: schneider-electric.com, product pages).
   Vulnerability for this ICP: deployment scale/cost oversized for a single
   mid-size plant; sold through long enterprise cycles.
2. **Spacewell Energy (Dexma)** — category: energy management SaaS for
   buildings/portfolios. Promise: analytics across many sites. ICP: ESCOs,
   facility managers, retail portfolios. Strength: mature product, partner
   channel (source: spacewell.com). Vulnerability: building/FM-centric —
   weaker fit for production-line manufacturing loads.
3. **Wattabit** — category: Spanish energy monitoring SaaS. Closest local
   analog. Strength: local, flexible. Vulnerability: generalist (buildings
   + industry), limited audit/ISO framing visible on site — estimate from
   public content, flagged `assumption`.
4. **Electrical integrators (composite)** — they bundle "a dashboard" with
   metering work at near-zero software price. Strength: already inside the
   plant, trusted by maintenance. Vulnerability: dashboards without alerts,
   analysis or audit outputs; abandoned post-handover (source: pattern
   across integrator sites; graded inference).

## Positioning map
**Axes that actually differentiate here:** (1) deployment weight — suite
projects vs. days-to-live SaaS; (2) audience — corporate energy teams vs.
plants without an energy manager; (3) job framing — "sustainability
platform" vs. "cost + audit compliance tool".
**Occupied:** giants own heavy/corporate/sustainability; integrators own
cheap/local/dashboard; Dexma owns portfolios/FM.
**Fluxen today:** invisible — no claimed category, website reads like a
generic IoT tool (source: declared site copy).
**[GAP]** "Audit-ready energy intelligence for mid-size plants without an
energy manager" — relevant (audit pain is acute and dated), open (nobody
claims the no-energy-manager plant explicitly), reachable (14 live plants,
92% retention, days-to-deploy are real evidence).
**[GAP HYPOTHESIS]** "Multi-plant standardization for industrial groups" —
attractive, but Dexma and the giants can credibly claim it; needs proof
Fluxen doesn't yet have.

## Category dynamics
EU energy-cost volatility and CSRD/ISO 50001 pressure are raising the
floor of demand; "energy efficiency as compliance" language is growing in
sector press. Suites keep moving upmarket toward sustainability reporting —
widening the mid-market gap rather than closing it.

## Positioning hypotheses (for Step 3)
- **H1 — The audit-ready EMS for plants without an energy manager.** Rides
  the `[GAP]`; compliance-dated urgency fights "do nothing".
- **H2 — Days-to-live energy intelligence (anti-suite).** Deployment-weight
  axis; risk: "easy" is claimed by everyone, weakly defensible alone.
- **H3 — The energy copilot that pays for itself in found waste.** ROI
  framing; risk: requires savings proof points Fluxen hasn't systematized —
  `unverified-claim` until win interviews land.
