# Demand Plan — Fluxen
*Go2Rev GTM Planner · Step 4 of 5 · (sample output, fictional company)*

**Sources used:** web benchmarks (cited) + user-declared funnel estimates.
HubSpot/Ahrefs/Similarweb not connected; internal numbers are declared.

## Executive summary
The goal (8 new plants ≈ +€150k ARR) with a 2–4 month cycle and ~1-in-4
proposal close means roughly **32–40 qualified opportunities in 12
months** — Fluxen generated ~20 last year, mostly from a saturated founder
network. The design shifts origination to three repeatable engines the
current team can actually run — systematized customer referrals,
audit-deadline ABM, and a compliance-content capture engine — with paid
amplification and events deliberately parked. Constraint honored: the
founder can only carry 4 parallel opportunities, so the plan staggers
generation and forces qualification discipline rather than raw volume.

## Design principles
1. **Trigger over volume.** Chase plants with dated audits, not "all
   manufacturers".
2. **Founder capacity is the bottleneck** — qualify hard, stagger waves.
3. **Everything must run on ≤€4k/month and no marketing hire.**

## Engine architecture (4 selected, discards shown)
| Engine | Funnel stage | First signal |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Customer referral system | Conversion (pre-qualified) | <60 days |
| 2. Audit-deadline ABM | Consideration → conversion | 60–90 days |
| 3. Compliance content capture (ISO 50001) | Capture (existing intent) | 90–150 days |
| 4. Integrator co-selling pilot | Consideration | 90–180 days |

Engines feed each other: content arms ABM sequences and referral
conversations; integrators and customers point to plants with dated audits;
ABM warms the accounts content captures.

**Discarded (visible):** paid search — CPC on EMS terms €6–12 (WordStream
industrial benchmarks 2025) burns the budget for ~2 clicks/day; trade-show
sponsorships — cost per qualified meeting far above referral path at this
budget; cold email at scale — generic industrial reply rates <1% (Belkins
2025), and the ICP responds to specificity, not sequence volume.

### Engines in detail (abridged for sample)
**1. Customer referral system.** 14 plants, 92% retention, zero systematic
asks today. Mechanism: post-first-audit-success referral ask + peer-plant
intro incentive (service credit). Health: asks made/month, intro→opp rate,
referral share of pipeline. Requires: CS + founder scripts. `needs-benchmark`:
sector-specific referral conversion — track own data from month one.
**2. Audit-deadline ABM.** List of 120 plants (public ISO registries +
certification-body announcements) filtered to certifications expiring in
6–12 months; founder-signed 3-touch sequences on the audit frame. Health:
list coverage, reply rate (B2B founder-led personalized: 5–12%, Lavender
benchmark 2025 — sector-generic, flagged), meetings/month vs. capacity 4.
**3. Compliance content capture.** 6–8 assets answering what plants search
before audits (ISO 50001 checklist, non-conformity teardowns, report
templates). Health: asset→session→contact conversion (bottom-funnel 2–6%,
generic benchmark); leads with dated audits. Honest note: domain is young —
this engine compounds into year 2, it does not carry year 1.
**4. Integrator co-selling pilot.** 3 integrators whose dashboards die
post-handover: they keep the metering work, Fluxen takes the software layer.
2-deal pilot before formalizing. Health: intros/quarter, pilot deals closed.

| Integrator partnerships | 4 | Medium | 90d+ | ~€0 |

## Activation sequence
**0–90 days:** engines 1+2 live; engine 3 in production; engine 4 scoping.
**90–180:** content live; integrator pilot; second ABM wave on engaged
accounts. **180–365:** double down on whichever of engines 1/2 shows
opp-rate traction; formalize integrator program if pilot closes ≥1 deal.

## What this plan does NOT solve
Qualification criteria and handoff discipline (the founder-capacity
bottleneck makes this urgent), the sales process itself, and funnel
instrumentation in Pipedrive — all mapped in Execution Readiness.
