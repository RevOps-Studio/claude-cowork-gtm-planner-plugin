# Positioning Architecture — Fluxen
*Go2Rev GTM Planner · Step 3 of 5 · (sample output, fictional company)*

## Executive summary
Fluxen's honest, defensible category is **audit-ready energy intelligence
for plants without an energy manager**. It converts the company's real
evidence (14 plants, days-to-deploy, auditor-accepted reports) into the
exact frame its ICP already thinks in — compliance deadlines and an energy
cost line nobody owns — and repositions the giants' strength (suite scale)
as the buyer's problem rather than Fluxen's weakness.

## Category decision
| Candidate | Space | Saturation | Capability fit | ICP appeal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A. Fast-deploy EMS (anti-suite) | Large | High ("easy" is claimed by all) | High | Medium |
| **B. Audit-ready energy intelligence for plants without an energy manager** | **Mid, growing (CSRD/ISO pressure)** | **Low — unclaimed** | **Very high (it is what the 14 plants bought)** | **Very high (their actual trigger)** |
| C. Energy copilot / ROI-found-waste | Mid | Medium (AI-copilot noise) | Medium (proof not systematized) | High if proven |

**Decision: B**, absorbing A as a pillar and C as a future proof-point
motion. Implication: Fluxen competes against "spreadsheets + audit panic",
not against Schneider's feature list. It gives up the corporate
sustainability-platform conversation deliberately.

## Core promise
**Canonical:** "Pass every energy audit and own your energy costs — without
hiring an energy manager."
**Fallback:** "Energy intelligence your plant can run without a specialist."
Credible because: outcome-framed, differential against every mapped player
(none addresses the no-specialist plant), and backed by live evidence
(auditor-accepted reports across 14 plants).

## Pillars (each shown passing the three-part test)
1. **Audit-ready by design.** Reports auditors accept, mapped to ISO 50001.
   *Relevant:* audits are dated, budgeted pain. *Differential:* integrator
   dashboards and utility portals don't produce them. *Defensible:* 14
   plants' audit history. → decisive pillar.
2. **Live in days on your existing meters.** No new hardware, no project.
   *Relevant:* plants fear production disruption. *Differential:* suites
   are months-long projects. *Defensible:* real deployment record.
3. **Alerts that find money, not dashboards that decorate.** Anomaly
   detection on consumption patterns. *Relevant:* energy cost line.
   *Differential:* integrator dashboards are passive. *Defensible:* partial —
   savings cases exist but unsystematized → `unverified-claim`, medium
   confidence, collect 3 quantified cases.
4. *(No fourth pillar — three that pass beat four that wobble.)*

## Segment translations
**Primary — plant manager, mid-size manufacturer:** promise in their words:
"the audit stops being a fire drill, and the energy line finally has an
owner — without a new hire." Heavy pillars: 1, 2. Vocabulary: audit
non-conformities, production continuity, cost per unit produced.
**Secondary — industrial groups (3–10 plants):** "standardize energy
visibility across plants without a corporate rollout." Pillars: 2, 3.
Held as expansion motion, not lead segment (see `[GAP HYPOTHESIS]`).

## Messages per funnel stage
**Awareness:** "Mid-size plants fail energy audits for the same three
reasons — none of them needs an energy manager to fix."
**Consideration:** "We'll show you, on your own meter data, what an auditor
would flag — in a 30-minute session."
**Decision:** "Your next audit is dated. Live in days, first report in
week one — or you walk away."

## Anti-messages bank
No "sustainability platform / ESG suite" language (funds the giants'
frame). No "easiest EMS" as lead claim (everyone says it; keep it as pillar
2 proof, not headline). No AI-copilot framing without systematized savings
proof (`unverified-claim`). No leading with price against integrators — the
fight is value vs. do-nothing, not cost vs. dashboards.

## What this changes tomorrow
Homepage hero → the canonical promise + audit framing. First two minutes of
the founder's pitch → open with the dated audit and the unowned cost line,
not with the product tour. Content that fits: audit-failure teardowns,
ISO 50001 checklists, savings cases. Content that clashes: generic IoT and
sustainability thought-pieces.
