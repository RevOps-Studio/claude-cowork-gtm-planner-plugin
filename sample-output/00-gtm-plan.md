# GTM Plan — Fluxen
*Built with the Go2Rev methodology · (sample output, fictional company)*

> A GTM plan is a chain of decisions: category → promise → engines →
> channels → commitments. Each decision builds on the one before.

## 1. Executive summary
Fluxen (energy-management SaaS, €900k ARR, 14 plants, founder-led) wants 8
new mid-size manufacturing plants in 12 months while breaking founder
dependence on origination. The category bet: **audit-ready energy
intelligence for plants without an energy manager** — deliberately walking
away from the sustainability-platform conversation the giants own, and
competing instead against spreadsheets and audit panic. Promise: *"Pass
every energy audit and own your energy costs — without hiring an energy
manager."* The math governs the design: 8 plants at ~25% proposal close on
a 2–4 month cycle demands 32–40 qualified opportunities (vs. ~20 last
year), generated by three engines the current team can run — systematized
customer referrals, audit-deadline ABM on ~120 trigger-qualified accounts,
and a compliance-content capture engine that compounds into year 2 — plus
an integrator co-selling pilot. First 90 days commit 6 initiatives, gated
by the founder's 4-parallel-opportunities capacity.

## 2. Business snapshot *(condensed — full version: 01)*
Mid-size-manufacturing EMS, €12–30k/plant/year, 92% retention, ICP = plants
50–500 employees with ISO 50001 pressure and no energy manager. Funnel:
~20 opps/year, ~60% founder network (saturated), no instrumented CRM.
Constraints: ≤€4k/month marketing, no marketing hire, founder capacity = 4
parallel opportunities.

## 3. Market map *(condensed — full version: 02)*
Giants (Schneider, Siemens) own the corporate suite frame; integrators own
cheap dashboards that die post-handover; Dexma owns portfolios/FM. The real
rival is layer 3: spreadsheets + do-nothing-until-the-audit. `[GAP]`:
audit-ready EMS for the no-energy-manager plant. `[GAP HYPOTHESIS]` parked:
multi-plant standardization (needs proof).

## 4. Positioning *(full version: 03)*
Category B confirmed at gate. Promise as above. Three pillars, each passing
the relevance/differentiation/defensibility test: audit-ready by design ·
live in days on existing meters · alerts that find money (supports, never
leads — `unverified-claim` until 3 quantified cases land). Anti-messages:
no ESG-platform language, no "easiest EMS" headline, no AI-copilot claims,
no price war with integrators.

## 5. Demand plan *(full version: 04)*
Four engines — referral system, audit-deadline ABM, compliance content,
integrator co-selling pilot — with paid search, trade shows and cold email
at scale visibly discarded on benchmark economics. Channel mix runs on
~€2k/month plus founder time. Staggered activation honoring the capacity
bottleneck.

## 6. Roadmap *(full version: 05)*
6 initiatives in 90 days (owners + result-milestones), 12-month outline
with a data-gated scale/hire decision in Q4. Parked list with reasons.

## 8. Execution readiness
This plan decides positioning and demand; it does not build the machinery
that converts and operates them. **Conversion design** — qualification
criteria, routing and founder-capacity rules, pipeline stages, sales
playbook; skipped → engines feed a bottleneck and the plan gets blamed for
a conversion problem (scheduled Q3). **Deployment** — content production
cadence, ABM operations, partner-program mechanics; skipped → engines stall
on the first busy month. **Measurement** — source/stage instrumentation
(roadmap #1 is the prerequisite), reporting cadence, engine-level review;
skipped → by month 3 budget follows opinions. Executing these three
workstreams requires either internal revenue-operations capacity or a
specialized partner; the roadmap assumes founder time, one CS lead and
freelance content — that gap is the thing to audit internally.
