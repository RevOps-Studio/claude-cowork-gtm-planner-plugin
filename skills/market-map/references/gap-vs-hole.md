# Gap vs. Hole — some spaces are empty for a reason

An empty position in the market is not automatically an opportunity.
Before labeling anything a `[GAP]`, run this test.

## The three-part test

1. **Relevant** — does the ICP actually care about this position? Would it
   change a buying decision, or is it differentiation only insiders notice?
2. **Open** — is it genuinely unclaimed? Check narrative claims, not just
   products: a competitor may own the position in buyers' minds without
   naming it.
3. **Reachable** — can THIS company credibly claim it with its current
   capabilities and evidence? A gap the company cannot defend within a
   quarter or two is someone else's gap.

## Labels

- Passes all three → `[GAP]`, with the argument for each part.
- Fails or is uncertain on any part → `[GAP HYPOTHESIS]`, with the specific
  doubt stated and what evidence would resolve it.

## Common holes that are not gaps

- **The premium corner nobody buys** — the market may have decided the
  category does not command premium pricing.
- **The "all-in-one" position in a specialist market** — buyers may be
  choosing this category precisely to avoid suites.
- **A position a big player abandoned** — find out why before moving in.
- **Positions that require evidence the company does not have** — a claim
  without proof is a liability, not a position.
