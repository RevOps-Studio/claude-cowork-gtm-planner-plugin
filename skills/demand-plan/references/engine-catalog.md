# Engine Catalog

Common B2B demand engine types. Descriptions are deliberately brief — the
work is screening them against THIS company's positioning, capacity and
speed requirements, not picking favorites.

- **Inbound SEO/content** — capture existing search demand with content
  mapped to buying intent. Slow to start (2–6 months), compounds. Requires
  consistent production capacity.
- **Thought leadership** — a named voice (usually founder) builds category
  authority on one platform. Cheap in cash, expensive in discipline.
- **Outbound / ABM** — targeted account lists, personalized sequences,
  multi-touch. Fast signal (weeks). Requires list quality and real
  personalization capacity — not spray volume.
- **Referral systems** — systematizing what today happens by luck: explicit
  asks, partner incentives, customer moments engineered for introductions.
  Often the cheapest engine a services company is not running.
- **Partnerships / channel** — other companies with the same buyer carry the
  message. Slow to establish, durable once running.
- **Owned events** — webinars, roundtables, small executive dinners. Strong
  for consideration and qualification in high-ticket B2B.
- **Third-party events** — industry conferences, sponsorships, speaking.
  Buys awareness and meetings; expensive per lead, fast to test.
- **Paid amplification** — paid media amplifying proven messages and assets.
  In most B2B, paid works as an amplifier of what already converts, not as a
  standalone engine.
- **Community-led** — building or hosting the space where the ICP gathers.
  Long horizon, deep moat. Rarely a first engine.
- **Product-led signals** — free tools, assessments, calculators that
  generate qualified intent. Requires build capacity; strong qualification.

## Screening reminder

Run every candidate through the triple fit (positioning coherence ×
operational viability × speed to first result) and require the selected set
to cover the full funnel. Typical healthy outcome: one fast engine
(outbound, events), one compounding engine (SEO, thought leadership), one
conversion/qualification engine (events, product-led), plus referrals
systematized.
