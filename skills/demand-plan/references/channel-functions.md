# Channel Functions

Channels are chosen for their function in the funnel, not by fashion.
Every channel in the mix declares exactly one primary function.

- **Awareness** — the ICP discovers the category and the company exists:
  LinkedIn organic, PR, podcasts, third-party events, thought leadership
  distribution.
- **Consideration** — the ICP evaluates the approach: blog/resource content,
  webinars, newsletters, comparison and methodology pages.
- **Capture** — existing intent finds the company: SEO, search ads, landing
  pages, lead magnets, directories.
- **Activation** — contacts become conversations: email sequences,
  retargeting, outbound touches, event follow-up.
- **Reactivation** — dormant contacts return: nurture tracks, win-back
  sequences, periodic flagship content.

## Mix rules

- 3–6 channels. Depth beats spread in B2B: four channels operated well
  outperform ten operated superficially.
- Each channel serves a named engine. A channel serving no engine is a cost.
- Declare organic vs. paid proportion per channel, with the reasoning.
  Neither is inherently better; paid usually amplifies what organic proved.
- Phase the mix: first 90 days (1–3 channels max), next quarter, later —
  with explicit conditions for each later activation.
