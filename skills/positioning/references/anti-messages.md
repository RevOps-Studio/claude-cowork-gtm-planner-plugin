# The Anti-Messages Bank

Positioning is also defined by what you refuse to say. The bank has three
sections; all three are mandatory.

## 1. What we do not say
Generic formulations that dilute the position: agency jargon, catch-all
claims ("end-to-end", "all-in-one", "your trusted partner"), and anything
that could be pasted onto any competitor's site without anyone noticing.

## 2. Competitor lines we do not imitate
From the Market Map: the phrases and frames competitors own. Repeating them
funds the competitor's position. If a competitor owns "fastest", the answer
is not "we're fast too" — it is a different axis.

## 3. Claims we cannot defend yet
Statements that would differentiate but lack evidence today. Register them
explicitly so they do not leak into copy by inertia. Each one is an
`unverified-claim` open question with a note on what proof would unlock it.

## Why this section earns its place
Teams drift. New hires, freelancers and agencies fill silence with generic
claims. The anti-messages bank is the cheapest guardrail in the whole plan:
it converts positioning discipline from taste into a checkable list.
