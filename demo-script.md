# Demo script — 60–90 seconds

Goal of the demo: show **method**, not speed. The viewer should leave
thinking "it forces the right decisions in the right order", not "it writes
fast".

**Setup (before recording):** plugin installed, no connectors, a fictional
company one-liner ready.

1. **(0–10s) Launch.** Type:
   `/gtm-planner:plan Fluxen — energy-management SaaS for mid-size plants, €900k ARR, Spain`
   Voiceover: "One command. No connectors, no data prep."
2. **(10–25s) Source readiness check.** Show the planner listing available
   sources and offering the fallback choice. Voiceover: "First thing it
   does: tells you what it knows, what it doesn't, and lets you choose."
3. **(25–45s) A checkpoint.** Answer one intake question; show a
   confidence-graded open question appear ("Is 'easiest to deploy' the real
   buying reason? — Low confidence, founder belief only"). Voiceover: "It
   never fills gaps with fiction — assumptions are graded and tracked."
4. **(45–65s) The category gate.** Show the category decision table and the
   explicit confirmation request. Voiceover: "The most consequential
   decision is a gate, not a paragraph. You decide; it argues."
5. **(65–90s) The ending.** Scroll the final plan: 90-day roadmap with
   owners and result-milestones → assumption & evidence ledger → Execution
   Readiness. Voiceover: "And it ends honestly: what the plan commits to,
   what it assumes, and what it deliberately doesn't cover."
